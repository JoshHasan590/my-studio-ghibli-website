What is Anime?

Anime, whilst being an abbreviation of the word “animation”, is more colloquially used to refer to animations produced in Japan. Often painstakingly hand-drawn, the art style is highly distinctive and rich with detail. It is important to note that anime has a vast breadth of content, both television and film, covering all genres. Much like traditional live-action film, anime also targets all audiences, children and adults alike. Anime productions have inspired countless works worldwide, for example imagery in Ghost in the Shell (1995) and The Matrix (1999), including the iconic green code rain.

What is Studio Ghibli?

Founded in 1985 and based in Tokyo, Studio Ghibli are best known for their animated feature films and have released over twenty works to date. Spanning a variety of unique stories and styles, many of the films follow a young protagonist’s coming-of-age and inner-growth throughout their respective plights. For the most part, the films can be considered family-friendly. However, that isn’t to say the creators dilute the messages or themes of their films. Japanese traditionalism and environmentalism are regularly present, as is the cost and horrors of war. Two of Ghibli’s most notable films are the harrowing Grave of the Fireflies (1988) and the Academy Award winning Spirited Away (2001).

Studio Ghibli & Disney

An apt comparison for Studio Ghibli is that of Disney and their classic feature films from the 1900s. In fact, Disney and Ghibli have enjoyed decades of collaboration. In 1996, Disney became the worldwide distributer of Studio Ghibli’s films, as well as producing high-quality English language dubs of the films. Notable cast members include: Patrick Stewart, Christian Bale, Daisy Ridley, Anne Hathaway and Matt Damon. Although distribution rights slowly changed ownership throughout the 2010s, there is no doubting Disney’s impact on popularising Studio Ghibli and their films worldwide.
